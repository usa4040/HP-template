The Process

Creating a Damascus blade is a labor of intense patience. Fire, force, and finish converge to create a functional work of art.

1. The Fire

Steel billets are heated to over 2000°F until they glow with the color of the setting sun, ready to be shaped.

2. The Fold

Hammer and anvil compress the layers. The steel is folded, re-welded, and drawn out repeatedly to build structure.

3. The Etch

After grinding and tempering, the blade is etched in acid to reveal the striking contrast of the Damascus pattern.
